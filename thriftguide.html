Thrift Guide

Understanding Sizing

Thrifted clothing comes from different eras and brands, so sizing can vary significantly. Here's what you need to know:

Size tags aren't everything: A vintage medium might fit like today's small. Always check the measurements we provide for each item.

Key measurements to know:

  • Chest/Bust: Measure around the fullest part
  • Waist: Measure around your natural waistline
  • Hips: Measure around the fullest part
  • Length: For tops, measure from shoulder to hem; for bottoms, measure from waist to hem
  • Inseam: For pants, measure from crotch to ankle

Pro tip: Keep your measurements handy in your phone. Compare them to our listed measurements before purchasing.

Vintage sizing: Pre-1980s clothing often runs smaller. A vintage size 12 might be closer to a modern size 6-8.

Condition Grading System

We carefully inspect every item and grade it honestly so you know exactly what you're getting.

Like New / Excellent

  • No visible flaws
  • Minimal to no signs of wear
  • May still have original tags
  • Looks and feels nearly brand new

Very Good

  • Gently used with very minor wear
  • No stains, holes, or significant flaws
  • Might have slight fading or minimal pilling
  • Still in great wearable condition

Good

  • Normal signs of wear from regular use
  • May have minor imperfections (small spots, light fading, minor pilling)
  • All flaws are noted in the description
  • Plenty of life left

Fair

  • Visible wear and imperfections
  • May have small holes, stains, or repairs
  • All issues clearly described and photographed
  • Great for DIY projects or casual wear

Note: We describe all flaws honestly in each listing. Check photos carefully and read descriptions thoroughly.

Thrift Shopping Tips

Start with an open mind: You never know what treasures you'll find. The best thrifted pieces are often unexpected discoveries.

Read descriptions carefully: We detail any flaws, fabric content, and special features. Don't skip this step.

Check photos closely: Zoom in on detail shots. We photograph items from multiple angles and highlight any imperfections.

Know your measurements: This is the #1 tip for successful online thrifting. When in doubt, size up - alterations are easier than trying to squeeze into something too small.
